<p><p>The melt-spun fiber market comprises two main types: filament melt-spun fibers and staple melt-spun fibers. Filament melt-spun fibers are continuous strands produced through a process that melts polymer and extrudes it through spinnerets, creating long fibers ideal for fabrics and technical applications. Staple melt-spun fibers, on the other hand, are short fibers produced from cut filament, suitable for blending with other fibers and used in non-wovens, clothing, and various textile products. Both types offer versatility in various end-use applications.</p></p>
